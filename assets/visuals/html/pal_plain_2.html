Search trees

Different dictionaries for different workloads.

Balanced trees

  • AVL: stricter balance, O(log n).
  • Red-black: also O(log n), common in libraries.
  • Splay: amortized O(log n), good with repeated access.

Special trees

  • B/B+ trees: block storage and database indexes.
  • k-d tree: geometric points in low dimensions.
  • Skip list: randomized ordered dictionary.
Exam sentence: All balanced search trees aim for logarithmic height; the difference is how they maintain balance and what workload they are optimized for.
5

Automata and text search

A finite state machine remembers how much of the pattern has matched.

Automata

  • DFA: exactly one next state.
  • NFA: a set of possible states.
  • Regex, DFA, and NFA describe regular languages.

Text search

  • KMP: exact matching in O(n + m).
  • Levenshtein DP: approximate matching in O(mn).
  • Aho-Corasick: many patterns in one scan.
Exam sentence: KMP can be seen as an automaton over prefix lengths: after a mismatch, failure links say how much of the prefix is still usable.

How to study PAL in two days

First pass

Read these two pages and say each exam sentence out loud. You only need the rough map first.

Second pass

Go into the notes and attach details to each box: definitions, algorithms, and complexity.

Final pass

Cover the notes and answer from memory: "what is it, what algorithm, what complexity, what trap?"

Three common traps

  • Do not mix directed and undirected Euler conditions.
  • Graph invariants can disprove isomorphism, but usually do not prove it.
  • O(1) matrix edge lookup is bought with O(n^2) memory.
