KO in Plain English

KO is about turning discrete choices into optimization problems. For each topic, know the model, whether it is easy or hard, and the standard exact, approximate, or graph algorithm.

1. Model the decision.Variables, constraints, objective.
2. Pick the method.Exact, polynomial graph algorithm, approximation, or DP.
3. State the guarantee.Optimal, feasible, pseudo-polynomial, or approximate.

ILP and Branch & Bound

Use variables to describe choices.

  • LP is polynomial; general ILP is NP-hard.
  • Binary variables are yes/no switches.
  • Branch & Bound splits cases and prunes by bounds.

Shortest paths and flows

Polynomial graph problems with strong structure.

  • Dijkstra: nonnegative edges.
  • Bellman-Ford: negative edges, detects negative cycles.
  • Max-flow min-cut links augmenting paths to cuts.

Knapsack and TSP

Hard problems where DP and approximation matter.

  • Knapsack DP is pseudo-polynomial.
  • FPTAS scales profits for approximation.
  • Metric TSP has double-tree and Christofides.

Scheduling

Assign jobs to time/resources.

  • Use Graham notation to describe the problem.
  • Know Bratley, Horn, list scheduling, McNaughton.
  • ILP models use start/order/time-indexed variables.

CSP and AC3

Reduce variable domains by consistency.

  • CSP = variables, domains, constraints.
  • Arc consistency removes unsupported values.
  • AC3 repeats revisions until stable or empty domain.

How to answer

Start with the model; then discuss algorithmic status.

  • What are the variables?
  • What is optimized?
  • Is the method exact, approximate, or heuristic?
Exam sentence: ILP is powerful because it models discrete choices with linear constraints, but integrality makes it NP-hard; Branch & Bound solves it by relaxing, branching, and pruning.

Three traps

  • Pseudo-polynomial does not mean polynomial in input length.
  • Dijkstra needs nonnegative edge weights.
  • Approximation ratios depend on minimization vs. maximization.
