LUP in Plain English

LUP is about proving by search. Convert formulas into a form a machine can search, then either find a model or derive a contradiction.

1. Normalize.CNF, clauses, Skolem form.
2. Search.DPLL/CDCL, SLD, resolution, saturation.
3. Interpret result.Model found, unsatisfiable, or proof by contradiction.

SAT and CNF

Boolean proving becomes clause search.

  • CNF = conjunction of clauses.
  • Tseitin avoids exponential CNF blowup.
  • Resolution derives new clauses until contradiction.

DPLL/CDCL

Modern SAT solving is smart backtracking.

  • Unit propagation forces assignments.
  • Backtracking explores choices.
  • Clause learning records why conflict happened.

Prolog and SLD

Logic programs answer goals by resolution.

  • Horn clauses have at most one positive literal.
  • SLD resolves selected goal with program rules.
  • Negation as failure means "not provable".

First-order resolution

Variables require unification and Skolemization.

  • Skolemization removes existential variables.
  • Unification finds substitutions that make terms equal.
  • Saturation keeps deriving clauses until done or contradiction.

Equality and models

Equality needs special handling; model search grounds domains.

  • Paramodulation/superposition reason with equality.
  • Grounding finite domains reduces to SAT.
  • Subsumption removes redundant clauses.

How to answer

Say what representation the solver searches.

  • Formula to clauses.
  • Rules for deriving consequences.
  • Stopping condition: model or empty clause.
Exam sentence: Resolution proves unsatisfiability by deriving the empty clause; in first-order logic this needs unification so clauses can match after substitution.

Three traps

  • CNF conversion and Tseitin encoding are not the same thing.
  • Skolemization preserves satisfiability, not logical equivalence.
  • Negation as failure is procedural, not classical negation.
