SSU in Plain English

SSU is about learning a predictor from data. The big story is: define loss, minimize training loss, control generalization, and understand the main model families.

1. Define the objective.Risk, empirical risk, likelihood, or loss.
2. Pick the model.SVM, probabilistic model, EM, neural network.
3. Explain training.Optimization plus generalization.

ERM and generalization

Training error is not the same as true error.

  • Risk = expected loss on the real distribution.
  • Empirical risk = average loss on the sample.
  • Bounds explain when low training loss transfers.

VC dimension

A capacity measure for hypothesis classes.

  • High capacity can fit more patterns.
  • Too much capacity risks overfitting.
  • Consistency means convergence to optimal risk.

SVMs

Find a separating hyperplane with maximum margin.

  • Hard margin: separable data.
  • Soft margin: allows violations with penalty.
  • Kernels make nonlinear boundaries via inner products.

MLE and EM

Fit probabilistic models by maximizing likelihood.

  • MLE chooses parameters that make data likely.
  • EM handles hidden variables.
  • E-step estimates hidden assignments; M-step updates parameters.

Deep networks

Layered differentiable functions trained by gradients.

  • Backprop computes gradients through layers.
  • SGD updates parameters from mini-batches.
  • CNNs use convolution for spatial structure.

How to answer

Tie every method to loss and optimization.

  • What is minimized or maximized?
  • What are the parameters?
  • How do we avoid overfitting?
Exam sentence: ERM minimizes training loss, but learning theory asks when this also gives low true risk; VC dimension and concentration bounds control that gap.

Three traps

  • Empirical risk is measured on the sample; true risk is an expectation over the unknown distribution.
  • EM increases a likelihood lower bound, not by magic "guessing".
  • Backprop computes gradients; SGD is the optimizer using them.
