Module 1

Propositional normal forms, SAT, DPLL, CDCL, and SMT

This is the Boolean proving core. CNF makes formulas solver-friendly, resolution proves unsatisfiability, DPLL searches with propagation, CDCL learns from conflicts, and lazy SMT adds a theory solver behind the Boolean layer.

Committee priorityHeavy focus

SAT and SMT are the search-engine layer behind LUP model search and automated proving, so they are high-yield under Šir's logic/knowledge-representation signal.

Say this out loud

What you must be able to say

  • NNF pushes negations to literals; CNF is a conjunction of disjunctive clauses; DNF is a disjunction of conjunctions.
  • Tseitin introduces auxiliary variables and gives a linear-size equisatisfiable CNF, not necessarily equivalent over the old variables alone.
  • Resolution derives A or B from A or x and B or not x; deriving the empty clause proves unsatisfiability.
  • Unit propagation removes satisfied clauses and falsified opposite literals; DPLL adds recursive branching.
  • CDCL builds an implication graph, analyzes conflicts, learns clauses, and backjumps non-chronologically.
  • Lazy SMT abstracts theory atoms to Booleans, asks SAT for an assignment, then asks a theory solver whether the theory literals are consistent.

Remember: SAT proves by finding an assignment or deriving contradiction; SMT repeats SAT guesses with a theory consistency check.

Module 2

Logic programming, Prolog, SLDNF, negation as failure, and cut

Prolog is proof search over Horn clauses. Declaratively, definite programs have a least Herbrand model; operationally, Prolog uses depth-first left-to-right SLD search, which is powerful but order-sensitive.

Committee priorityHeavy focus

Prolog connects relational logic to executable proof search, matching the committee's relational-logic emphasis.

  • Horn clauses and Herbrand least-model semantics
  • SLD and SLDNF operational proof search
  • negation as failure, green cut, and red cut

Say this out loud

What you must be able to say

  • A Horn clause has at most one positive literal; a definite clause has exactly one positive head literal.
  • The Herbrand universe contains all ground terms; the Herbrand base contains all ground atoms; an interpretation is a subset of the base.
  • Definite programs have a least Herbrand model, the least fixed point of the immediate consequence operator.
  • SLD resolution selects a goal atom, unifies it with a clause head, and replaces it with that clause body under the MGU.
  • Prolog's strategy is depth-first and left-to-right, so it can loop even when a logical answer exists.
  • Negation as failure succeeds when the positive goal finitely fails; cut commits to choices and can be green or red.

Remember: declarative Prolog asks what follows logically; operational Prolog asks what the search strategy actually tries first.

Say this out loud

What you must be able to say

  • Clause-form pipeline: eliminate implications, push negations, standardize variables, prenex, Skolemize, drop universals, convert matrix to CNF, split clauses.
  • Skolemization preserves satisfiability, not equivalence; existential witnesses become constants or functions of preceding universal variables.
  • Unification finds a substitution making terms equal; an MGU is the most general such substitution; occurs check blocks x = f(x).
  • FOL resolution resolves complementary literals after applying their MGU.
  • Subsumption removes clauses that are less general than another clause.
  • Saturation repeatedly adds consequences until it derives the empty clause or reaches a saturated non-redundant set; satisfiable FOL cases may not terminate.

Remember: Skolemization changes witnesses into new symbols, so it preserves satisfiability but not exact equivalence.

Module 4

Equality: axioms, paramodulation, and superposition

Equality can be encoded with axioms, but that creates a huge search space. Modern theorem provers reason with equality directly: replace equals by equals, order terms, and simplify by rewriting.

Committee prioritySolid focus

Equality is a natural extension of saturation proving, but the main exam payoff is the contrast between noisy axioms and direct equality inference.

  • reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, congruence, and substitutivity
  • paramodulation as replace-equals-by-equals inference
  • superposition, ordering, rewriting, and redundancy control

Say this out loud

What you must be able to say

  • Axiomatic equality adds reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, congruence for functions, and substitutivity for predicates.
  • The axiomatic approach is complete in principle but inefficient because it generates many equality clauses.
  • Paramodulation uses an equality s = t to replace a unifiable occurrence of s by t inside another clause.
  • Superposition is an ordered, refined equality calculus combining resolution, paramodulation-style equality reasoning, term ordering, and rewriting.
  • The exam contrast: do not just add equality axioms if you want an efficient automated prover.

Remember: equality axioms are logically enough but noisy; paramodulation and superposition make replacement a built-in inference step.

Module 5

Model search in generic domains by grounding and SAT encoding

Model search turns finite first-order structure into propositional search. Pick a finite domain, ground variables, encode atoms as Booleans, add constraints, and let SAT solve the finite problem.

Committee priorityHeavy focus

Grounding is the bridge from relational knowledge representation to SAT-based model search, which is a direct high-potential LUP exam pattern.

  • finite-domain grounding and ground-atom counts
  • SAT encodings for implication and exactly-one constraints
  • grounding blow-up, typing, symmetry breaking, and lazy grounding

Say this out loud

What you must be able to say

  • Grounding over a finite domain instantiates variables with domain objects.
  • Each ground atom becomes a propositional variable.
  • Common encodings: at least one, at most one, exactly one, and implication.
  • A predicate of arity k over n objects can create n^k ground atoms, so grounding can explode.
  • Mitigation includes typed domains, symmetry breaking, propagation before grounding, lazy grounding, compact cardinality encodings, and incremental SAT.

Remember: finite grounding turns first-order structure into a big propositional SAT problem.

Oral answer skeleton

Use this as the final answer spine. It keeps the question from turning into a soup of proof-system names.

Say it

  1. Start with propositional normal forms: NNF, CNF, DNF, Tseitin, SAT, and resolution refutation.
  2. Explain unit propagation, DPLL, CDCL clause learning, implication graph, and non-chronological backjumping.
  3. Explain lazy SMT as SAT over Boolean abstractions plus consistency checking by a theory solver.
  4. Switch to Prolog: Horn/definite clauses, Herbrand universe/base/interpretation, least Herbrand model, SLD resolution.
  5. Cover SLDNF, negation as failure, and cut, including why they affect operational behavior.
  6. Give the FOL clause-form pipeline, then unification, MGU, occurs check, FOL resolution, subsumption, and saturation.
  7. Compare equality axioms with paramodulation and superposition.
  8. Finish with finite-domain grounding and propositional SAT encodings for model search.
